## What I contributed to

I am an active member of the **IPNET CyberSec Club**, a structured cybersecurity training program that grew from zero to **60+ active members** spanning every academic level (L1 to M2). It is not a hobby club, but a structured learning environment focused on practical cybersecurity training.

Within the club, I contributed to the ecosystem built around an open-source foundation based on **[cyberstudent-ctf](https://github.com/cyberstudent-ctf)**, which has been extended with features such as mandatory writeups, tiered group management, anti-cheat enforcement, milestone role progression, and administrative tracking.

**Tech stack used in the ecosystem:**

| Layer | Technology | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Bot Framework | Sapphire Framework + Discord.js v14 | Event-driven, modular, production-grade |

| Deployment | Docker + PM2 | 24/7 uptime, zero-downtime restarts |

## What makes this ecosystem effective

### Mandatory writeup system
No points without a writeup: each member submits a technical report covering approach, tools used, exploitation steps, and remediation. This builds a real security portfolio over time.

### Three skill tiers
Members are split into `I-Am-New`, `I-ve-One-Year-Experience`, and `I-ve-More-Experience`. Each group receives calibrated challenges every Saturday, ensuring beginners progress without being overwhelmed.

### "Learn by teaching" requirement
Anyone who solves a challenge must present their method to the group. This reinforces technical clarity and communication skills that are essential in real-world cybersecurity roles.

### Club culture
- **Strict deadline policy**: writeup required by the following Saturday or temporary exclusion from scoring
- **Anti-cheat system**: monitoring of channels to prevent unauthorized flag sharing
- **Best Cyber Battle Woman award**: recognition of women in cybersecurity
- **Togo's first university-run CTF**: the [IPNET Cyber Battle](/posts/ipnet-cyber-battle/)
